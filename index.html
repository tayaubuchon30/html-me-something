My Time in Southeast Asia

A collection of memories from a semester abroad

    Bangkok | Hua Hin & Cha-am | Khao Sok National Park | Koh Tao | Phnom Penh Siem Reap & Angkor Wat

Bangkok

While studying at Webster U, I had the opportunity to study abroad at a location of my choice. Naturally I chose the furthest destination possible, Thailand.

Bangkok is a city of 8.3 million people, filling the capital with vibrant sights, smells, and experiences

Hua Hin & Cha-am

During my studies, I lived approximately 3 hours south of Bangkok on the Gulf Coast in Hua Hin.

Considered a popular, quiet vacation destination for Thais and Europeans, it was a quaint and beautiful place to call home

Khao Sok National Park

If you board an overnight sleeper bus for about $30 USD, you can make it to Khao Sok

There you'll find winding mountain roads, where you'll catch glimpses of elephant tails swaying back and forth behind the trees

Koh Tao

Further south, if you board one of the ferries, you'll make it to one of dozens of islands, Koh Tao.

This island came straight out of a postcard, with bungalows and dive shops on the beach.

If you venture to one of the local bars, you'll find a scrappy dog named "Ugly" and a fiesty Irish bartender serving backpackers and avid divers buckets of whiskey & RedBull

Phnom Penh

When travelling by land, you have to cross customs at the entrance to Cambodia from Thailand. I didn't spend much time here; but my experience provided interesting memories consisting of bribing border guards, and sharing a 2-hour cab ride with 2 Russian men that vaguely reminded me of Ren and Stimpy in Hawaiian shirts and fedoras

I also learned that when you say "vodka" to a Russian, you must say "wodka."

Siem Reap & Angkor Wat

Lastly, I made it to the town that hosts Angkor Wat, the ancient temple complex, Siem Reap. The town thrives off of Angkor tourism, and rightfully so (see evidence below).
